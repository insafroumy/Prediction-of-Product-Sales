# Prediction-of-Product-Sales
## Project Description
This project focuses on analyzing sales data for various retail products across different outlet types. The goal is to perform Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to understand the factors that drive sales performance, such as product price (MRP), item visibility, and outlet characteristics. By identifying these patterns, businesses can make data-driven decisions to optimize inventory and increase overall revenue.

##Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Insights
During the analysis, several visualizations were created to explore the dataset. Below are two key insights:

###1. Distribution of Sales (Histogram)
Interpretation: The histogram of Item Outlet Sales shows a right-skewed distribution. This indicates that most products have lower sales volumes (concentrated between 0 and 4,000), while only a small number of high-performing items achieve sales figures above 8,000.

###2. Feature Correlation (Heatmap)
Interpretation: The correlation heatmap reveals a moderate positive correlation (0.57) between Item_MRP (Maximum Retail Price) and Item_Outlet_Sales. This suggests that products with a higher list price generally contribute more to the total sales revenue generated at the outlets.
